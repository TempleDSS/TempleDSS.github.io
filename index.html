What Is the Scholars Studio?

The Loretta C. Duckworth Scholars Studio (LCDSS) is the digital scholarship center of Temple University Libraries, located inside Charles Library. Since its founding in 2014 as the Digital Scholarship Center (DSC) in Paley Library, it has grown into a multidisciplinary space where faculty, students, and researchers apply computational and creative methods to questions across the humanities, social sciences, sciences, and the arts.

The Scholars Studio provides one-on-one consultations, hands-on workshops, fellowship and residency programs, and access to specialized equipment—from 3D printers and laser cutters to VR headsets and media production tools. Its mission is to lower barriers to advanced digital methods and foster collaborative, experimental scholarship at every level of the university.

Over the past decade, the Scholars Studio has supported work spanning more than 35 academic disciplines, documented in an extensive blog archive of over 200 project write-ups, tutorials, and reflections. It has trained hundreds of graduate and undergraduate students in skills ranging from Python and GIS to photogrammetry and game design.

Programs & Fellowships

Faculty Fellowship Program

Semester-long fellowships supporting faculty in developing digital research or pedagogical projects with Scholars Studio staff.

Graduate Extern Program

Graduate students work alongside studio staff, gaining hands-on experience in digital methods while advancing their own research.

Faculty Learning Community

A cohort-based program (now in its fourth year) where faculty integrate advanced digital tools into their teaching practice.

Makerspace Residency

Artists and makers spend extended time in the makerspace creating work with digital fabrication, electronics, and emerging technology.

Cultural Analytics Practicum

A certificate-level program training students in computational approaches to cultural data, from text mining to image analysis.

Innovative Teaching with Makerspace Technology Grant

Funding for faculty to incorporate makerspace tools—3D printing, laser cutting, physical computing—into their courses.

LCDSS Game Jam

Annual event where students design and build original games in a compressed timeframe, blending creativity with technical skill.

Digital Scholars Program

An early initiative pairing graduate students with digital tools and mentorship to develop semester-long research projects.

Notable Projects Across a Decade

Below is a selection of standout projects produced at the Scholars Studio from 2014 to 2025, illustrating the breadth of disciplines, methods, and creative ambition the studio has supported.

2025

Building an Immersive Archive of Greek Orthodox Churches in Istanbul

Emily Neumeier · Art History / Architecture / Digital Humanities

A faculty-led project using 360° video and 3D modeling to create a navigable digital archive of overlooked Greek Orthodox religious monuments in Istanbul. The project demonstrates how immersive visualization can transform our understanding of architectural heritage that is difficult to access in person.

360° video 3D modeling cultural heritage
2025

The Untold History of Fletcher Street's Stables

Courtney Berne · History / Urban Studies / Geography

A multi-layered mapping project documenting the evolution of Black-run horse stables in North Philadelphia. Using GIS, archival research, and oral history, Berne traces how these stables have shaped and been shaped by urban change, creating a digital counter-narrative of a community often overlooked in official city records.

GIS mapping public history urban studies
2024

Tunnel of Love: The First Makerspace Residency

Ollie Goss · Sculpture / Visual Art

The inaugural makerspace residency produced an immersive sculptural installation exploring queerness, secrecy, and visibility through the aesthetic of an amusement-park dark ride. Goss combined laser-cut components, electronics, and hand-crafted elements to create an interactive physical experience, setting the standard for residencies to come.

sculpture electronics laser cutting critical making
2024

The Banned Books Project (3-Part Series)

SaraGrace Stefan, Sydney Grimm, Abigail Corcelli · Literary Studies / Digital Humanities

A collaborative, three-part computational text analysis initiative examining literary censorship from multiple angles: distant reading patterns across banned titles, visual rhetoric of banned book covers, and the mechanisms of censorship itself. The project, emerging from the Representation Lab, demonstrates how digital methods can illuminate systemic cultural forces.

text analysis distant reading data visualization
2024

Navigating the Western Movement of Artifacts: Mapping the Looted Benin Bronzes

Mikayla Gonzalez · History / Media Studies

A GIS-driven investigation of colonial artifact displacement, mapping the global journeys of the Benin Bronzes from their origins to museums and private collections worldwide. The project visualizes repatriation debates and the geographic dimensions of cultural heritage disputes.

mapping cultural heritage colonial history
2024

Rock TV: Makerspace Residency

Francesca Lally · Visual Art / Archaeology

An interdisciplinary project transforming vintage 1970s Fisher-Price Movie Viewers into hand-cranked cinema devices. Lally hand-painted Super 8 film sequences, laser-cut mirrored plexiglass panels inscribed with texts from Scottish geologists, and hand-typed a bound script on dura-lar film. The work was exhibited at Vox Populi's 2024 juried exhibition in Philadelphia.

laser cutting film sculpture exhibition
2024

Green Design Lab: Pre-College in the Makerspace

Hannah Tardie · Education / Sustainable Design

A two-week pilot course bringing pre-college students into the makerspace to combine sustainable design principles with 3D printing and digital fabrication. The program introduced high schoolers to iterative prototyping while centering environmental responsibility.

3D printing sustainability education digital fabrication
2023

Social Network Analysis of Abhidharma Buddhism Transmission in Early Medieval China

Lu Huang · Religion / Cultural Analytics

Applying computational social network analysis to centuries of Buddhist intellectual transmission, this project mapped how Abhidharma teachings traveled through networks of monks, translators, and institutions in early medieval China—revealing hidden patterns in the spread of religious knowledge.

network analysis religion cultural analytics
2023

Urban GIS, Land Cover, and Bees in the City

Austin Martin · Geography / Urban Studies / Ecology

Using remote sensing data and GIS spatial analysis, this project investigated urban landscapes as potential habitats for declining bee populations. By mapping land cover classifications across city environments, Martin demonstrated that cities can serve as unexpected havens for pollinators.

GIS remote sensing ecology R
2022

Visualizing Wikidata: Identity and Representation in Black Art Exhibitions

Synatra Smith, Jay Winkler, Rebecca Y. Bayeck · Art History / Cultural Studies / Digital Humanities

A linked open data project using Python to query and visualize Wikidata's representation of Black art exhibitions. The project interrogated what knowledge structures like Wikidata include, exclude, and how they encode identity—bridging digital scholarship with critical race studies.

linked open data Python Wikidata data visualization
2022

Visualizing Changes in Colombian Wetlands with ArcGIS Story Maps

Maria Victoria Sarmiento Giraldo · Geography / GIS

A spatial storytelling project documenting environmental land cover changes in Colombian wetland ecosystems using ArcGIS Story Maps. The work combined satellite imagery analysis with narrative to make ecological data accessible to wider audiences.

ArcGIS Story Maps GIS environmental studies
2022

Diggin' Philly: The Black Philadelphia Project

Tauheedah Asad · History / Urban Studies / Media

A digital cultural heritage initiative grounded in journalism that documents and preserves Black Philadelphia's built environment and community history. The two-part project explored digital practices for studying cultural heritage, combining mapping, oral history, and archival research.

cultural heritage mapping public history
2021

Creating a 3D Map of an Archaeological Site in Blender

Elysia Petras · Archaeology / History

A pioneering exploration of using the open-source 3D software Blender for historical archaeological research. Petras demonstrated how 3D terrain reconstruction can reveal spatial relationships invisible in traditional 2D site plans, creating new methods for landscape archaeology.

Blender 3D modeling archaeology landscape
2020

Arcade Our Way: Video Games and Toys for Social Change

Renee Jackson · Art Education / Gaming Studies

A faculty fellowship project culminating in an art exhibition (displayed through November 2021) that used video games and interactive toys as vehicles for social commentary. The project exemplified how game design and play can function as critical artistic practice.

game design art exhibition social change
2020

Measuring Impact of Built Environment on Health (4-Part Series)

Huilin Zhu · Geography / Economics / Computer Science

A four-part deep dive using machine learning and neural networks to study how built environments affect health outcomes—specifically obesity prevalence—across Pennsylvania. The project combined satellite imagery, transfer learning with Keras, and spatial analysis in a genuinely interdisciplinary approach.

machine learning neural networks GIS public health
2018

Virtual Blockson: An Accessible VR Experience

Jasmine Clark · Education / Digital Humanities

A multi-year initiative to create a virtual reality experience of Temple's Charles L. Blockson Afro-American Collection, with a sustained emphasis on accessibility. The project developed VR environments for history education while addressing how immersive technologies can be made inclusive for users with disabilities.

virtual reality accessibility cultural heritage education
2018

Viral Networks: Does Misinformation Spread Like a Virus?

Ania Korsunska · Information Science / Media Studies

A network analysis project mapping how scientific misinformation propagates through social media, tracing citation chains and sharing patterns to understand the lifecycle of false claims. The work anticipated broader public concern about misinformation ecosystems.

network analysis data visualization misinformation web scraping
2017

50 Years of Gen Con Events

Matt Shoemaker · History / Gaming Studies

A large-scale digital archive project documenting fifty years of Gen Con gaming convention programming. Built with Omeka, the publicly accessible archive (best50yearsingaming.com) became the foundation for the first dataset published in TUScholarShare, connecting gaming history with institutional data preservation.

Omeka digital archive gaming history TUScholarShare
2017

Building a New Wave Science Fiction Corpus

Alex Wermer-Colan · Literary Studies / Cultural Studies

An annual partnership between the Digital Scholarship Center and Special Collections to digitize and build a machine-readable corpus of New Wave science fiction texts for computational literary analysis. The project combined OCR, text cleaning, and corpus linguistics to enable new forms of literary scholarship.

corpus building text analysis OCR special collections
2017

Queer Encoding Conference

Digital Scholarship Center · Digital Humanities

The DSC co-sponsored "Queer Encoding: Encoding Diverse Identities," a conference on the Text Encoding Initiative that examined how digital encoding standards represent—or fail to represent—diverse identities. The event was live-streamed and its keynote presentations were archived online.

TEI conference digital humanities
2016

Mapping Ships Lost During the First World War

Andrea Siotto · Geography / History

A multi-part project combining web scraping (Python and C#), geocoding, and QGIS to map the locations and circumstances of naval losses during WWI. The series became a widely-read tutorial on the blog, walking readers through the full pipeline from data collection to animated geospatial visualization.

web scraping Python QGIS historical mapping
2015–2016

If Gardens Could Talk

Environmental Studies Graduate Students

An early critical making project using Arduino microcontrollers and Raspberry Pi sensors to give Philadelphia community gardens a "voice"—collecting environmental data and visualizing it through GIS. The project blended physical computing, environmental science, and community engagement, embodying the studio's ethos from its earliest days.

Arduino Raspberry Pi critical making environmental studies
2014–2015

#WatchingCosmos: Twitter Network Analysis

Angele M. Cirucci · Media and Communication Studies

One of the studio's earliest documented projects, this network analysis of the Twitter conversation around the Cosmos reboot combined Voyant text analysis with social network visualization to examine who was watching, participating, and shaping public science discourse on social media.

network analysis Twitter Voyant media studies
