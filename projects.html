A Decade of Digital Scholarship

These projects were selected to represent the full range of what the Scholars Studio makes possible: from immersive cultural heritage archives to network analyses of misinformation, from critical making sculptures to machine learning for public health. Each one pushed the boundaries of its discipline using digital methods supported by studio staff and equipment.

2025

Building an Immersive Archive of Greek Orthodox Churches in Istanbul

Emily Neumeier · Art History / Architecture / Digital Humanities

A faculty-led project using 360° video and 3D modeling to create a navigable digital archive of overlooked Greek Orthodox religious monuments in Istanbul. The project demonstrates how immersive visualization can transform understanding of architectural heritage that is difficult to access in person, preserving spaces threatened by neglect and urban development.

2025

The Untold History of Fletcher Street's Stables

Courtney Berne · History / Urban Studies / Geography

A multi-layered GIS mapping project documenting the evolution of Black-run horse stables in North Philadelphia. Combining archival research, spatial analysis, and oral history, Berne traces how these stables have shaped and been shaped by urban change, creating a digital counter-narrative for a community often overlooked in official city records.

2024

Tunnel of Love: The First Makerspace Residency

Ollie Goss · Sculpture / Visual Art

The inaugural makerspace residency produced an immersive sculptural installation exploring queerness, secrecy, and visibility through the aesthetic of an amusement-park dark ride. Goss combined laser-cut components, electronics, and hand-crafted elements to create an interactive physical experience that set the standard for all residencies to follow.

2024

Rock TV: Makerspace Residency

Francesca Lally · Visual Art / Archaeology

An interdisciplinary project transforming vintage 1970s Fisher-Price Movie Viewers into hand-cranked cinema devices. Lally hand-painted Super 8 film sequences, laser-cut mirrored plexiglass panels inscribed with texts from Scottish geologists, and hand-typed a bound script on dura-lar film. The work was exhibited at Vox Populi's 2024 juried exhibition in Philadelphia.
